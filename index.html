                A vibrant panchayat town and taluk headquarters in Coimbatore district, Tamil Nadu, known for its rich heritage and serene landscapes.

About Sulur

       
           
                               

Location & Geography

               

                    Sulur Town Panchayat is situated on the Eastern side of Coimbatore City, approximately 19 km away on the Coimbatore – Trichy (NH-67) National Highway. It is located at $11^\circ$ North latitude and $77^\circ$ East longitude, with an average elevation of 340 meters. The town is divided by the holy Noyyal River and is surrounded by Kangayampalayam, Kannampalayam, Irugur, and Kalangal Village Panchayats.                

               

Demographics & Administration

               

                    As per the 2011 India census, Sulur had a population of 27,909, with males and females constituting approximately 50% each. The average literacy rate is 78%, higher than the national average. Sulur is administered by an elected Panchayat Body and serves as the headquarters of the newly created Sulur Taluk.                

           
           
               

Town History & Etymology

               

                    The history of Sulur dates back approximately 2300 years, with ancient coins from the third century BC found here. During the ninth century AD, Adithya Cholan introduced agriculture and constructed the Sulur Reservoir. The town's name has evolved through time: it was known as `Suralur` until the ninth century, then `Ariyapirati Nallur` (9th-14th century), `Sundrapandiya Nallur` until the 18th century, and `Suranur` which eventually transformed into `Sulur`. Theories suggest the name might come from a type of sugarcane, Sural, or a previous ruler, Suranoor. Megalithic pottery (`Sulur dish`) from the first century is even displayed at the British Museum.                

               

Agriculture & Industries

               

                    Sulur plays a vital role in the region's economy with numerous cotton mills providing employment to thousands. It also hosts vibrant local markets: the `Sulur Sandhai` (சூலூர் சந்தை) on Fridays near the Noyyal River banks and the daily `Uzhavar Sandhai` (உழவர் சந்தை) near Balaji Hospital.                

           
       
   
       
       
           

Administration & Political Landscape

           
               

Sulur Assembly Constituency

               

                    The **Sulur Assembly constituency**, designated as **Constituency No. 116** for the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, is situated in the **Coimbatore district** of Tamil Nadu, India.                

               

                    It forms a crucial part of the **Coimbatore Lok Sabha constituency**. The constituency has a rich history, having been **first established in 1957** and subsequently **re-established in 2008**.                

               

                    As of the **2021 election**, Sulur Assembly constituency boasted **3,17,124 registered electors**. The current **Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA)** representing Sulur is **V. P. Kandasamy** of the **AIADMK party**, who was elected in 2021.

Sulur Lake & Boat House

                       

                            A picturesque lake featuring a popular boat house with motorized and pedal boats. It's also a great spot for nature observation.                        

                   
               
                               
                                       
                       

Sulur Science Park

                       

                            This park fosters scientific temper among youngsters with a dedicated library and profiles of 262 world-famous scientists.                        

                   
               
                               
                                       
                       

Sulur Air Force Station

                       

                            A historical Indian Air Force base established in 1940 by the British Raj, playing a strategic role in World War II.

How To Reach Sulur

           
                               
                   

Railway

                   

                        The nearest railway station is **Sulur Road Railway Station (Muthugoundarpudur)**, located just 3 km from Sulur.                    

               
                               
                   

Airport

                   

                        **Kangayampalayam - Sulur Military Air Force Station** is just 1 km away. The **Coimbatore International Civil Airport (Peelamedu)** is approximately 15-16 km from Sulur.                    

               
                               
                   

Bus Routes

                   

                        Sulur is well-connected by numerous bus routes from various parts of Coimbatore, including services like Ukkadam–Sulur Aero (19B, 30B, 30C), Gandhipuram–Sulur Aero (19C, 30J), and many others connecting to nearby villages and the city center.
